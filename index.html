ABOUT ME

I am a Ph.D. student in Human-Centered Computing at the Georgia Institute of Technology. My research broadly focuses on Human-Computer Interaction, Ubiquitous Computing, computational toolkits, and behavior change, with an emphasis on designing tangible, material, and multisensory interfaces that support everyday experiences. I am a member of the Uncommon Sense Lab and CoDe Craft under the advisory of Prof. Alex T. Adams and Prof. HyunJoo Oh. I have research experience in many labs, including the Morphing Matter Lab. the Media and Interaction Lab, the Future Lab, and the Pervasive HCI Group at Tsinghua University.

I design and study interactive systems that bridge physical materials and digital computation—from fluid and fiber-based displays to paper-based tactile interfaces and ambient lighting for dining experiences. I adopt user-centered and participatory methods, combining workshop facilitation, in-the-wild studies, and hands-on prototyping to explore how emerging materials and fabrication techniques can enable new forms of expression, accessibility, and wellbeing.

Before Georgia Tech, I received my M.F.A. in Art & Science from Tsinghua University (advised by Prof. Haipeng Mi) and my B.F.A. in Product Design from Beijing Institute of Fashion Technology. My work has been published at premier HCI venues including CHI and TEI, exhibited internationally, and supported by scholarships including the CMD-IT Tapia Celebration scholarship.

PROJECTS & CREATIVE WORK

Selected creative and research projects from my practice in interactive media, morphing matter, and installation art:

  • Tactile Compass — Tangible navigation interfaces for visually impaired people.
  • ViviPaint — Thermochromic toolkit for accessible dynamic painting.
  • Remaining Candle — New media art installation (Beijing, 2021).
  • Cosmusica / 宇宙八音盒 — Interactive sound and cosmic experience design.
